Why?

  • 19G OF CO2
    This is the equivalent carbon footprint of a 1Mo email sent to 1 recipient, this number grows to 73g for 10 recipients and to 615g for 100.
    5 E-MAILS
    5 e-mails are enough to emit as much CO2 as an average car driving for 1km, yet little to no consideration is given to their contribution.
    MORE IS COMING
    The digitalization of services and the exponential growth of the number of platforms owned per customer will keep on adding to the already gigantic flow of data.
    AWARENESS
    Dividing by 10 the number of recipients to our emails would allow to divide by 4 the carbon footprint of ou emails. Deleting them once read, limiting the size of our attachments are other easy means to bring down this forgotten source of CO2.

YOU CAN DO BETTER

Clean

There are very easy ways for you to reduce the impact your inbox has on the environment. By deleting your old messages that require servers running to be accessible at any time, unsubscribing from mailing lists or reducing the number of recipients and the size of the attachments to your emails, you can easily reduce the carbon footprint of your inbox.
